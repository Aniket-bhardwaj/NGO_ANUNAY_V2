FOUNDER

Colonel Satyavarat Bhardwaj, founder of Satya Shree Lakshmi Shanti Sansthan, exemplifies dedication through his remarkable journey. A decorated Army officer and pioneer in cryptography, he later focused on education and community development. After retirement, he established a computer training institute, aiding over 5000 students. His Sansthan, named in honor of his parents, upholds his commitment to education, honoring educators and improving infrastructure in Churi Ajitgarh. Colonel Bhardwaj’s legacy continues to inspire future generations, driving progress in education and community service.

OUR INSPIRATIONS

Guru Shanti Swaroop Ji, born in 1902, was a dedicated educator and revolutionary, inspired by Mahatma Gandhi. He left a British education job to promote social and educational reforms. His passion for teaching led to the establishment of schools in rural areas like Churi Ajitgarh, improving local infrastructure and education. His work is remembered for transforming lives through discipline, knowledge, and service. Villagers honored him with a statue and annual celebrations, ensuring his legacy in education and community upliftment continues.

VISION

Our vision is to cultivate and disseminate happiness throughout the country, touching the lives of all living beings. We strive to create self-sustaining development models for society, particularly in rural areas. By empowering the needy and affected people, we aim to improve their quality of life, ultimately bringing smiles and happiness to them and the nation as a whole.

MISSION

Our mission is to bring smiles and happiness by fostering self-sustaining developmental models for society, with a special focus on rural areas. We aim to empower the needy and affected by involving them in the formulation and implementation of initiatives that enhance their quality of life, thereby spreading joy and well-being across the nation.
